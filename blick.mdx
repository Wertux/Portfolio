At Blick.ch — Switzerland's leading news platform with a cross-media reach of 1.3 million — I grew from junior to a confident, independent designer across nearly two years. During that time I took on increasing ownership: rebuilding the design system from the ground up, establishing a continuous research practice from scratch, and shipping across web, iOS, and Android.

## Project Overview

**Organisation:** Ringier AG  
**Role:** UX Designer & Researcher  
**Timeline:** November 2022 – September 2024  
**Platforms:** Web (Blick.ch, Blick Sport), iOS, Android

## UX/UI Design

I designed across the full product surface — responsive web, iOS, and Android — spanning new features and major redesigns for both Blick.ch and Blick Sport. My work focused on translating user needs into clear, platform-appropriate interfaces: understanding where patterns could be shared across platforms and where they needed to diverge to meet native expectations.

Working closely with engineering, I developed a practical sense for the gap between design intent and implementation reality — learning to make decisions that held up in production, not just in Figma.

## Design System

The existing design system had drifted significantly from what was actually being built. Components were outdated, inconsistently applied, and not structured in a way that could scale across platforms.

I took ownership of key areas within a collaborative rebuild — working alongside my supervisor to establish an Atomic Design approach and break the system into composable, reusable building blocks. This brought the system back into alignment with the live product and gave the team a shared language for both web and native mobile components. I also put a maintenance process in place — clear guidelines for how components were created, updated, and governed — so the system could stay current as the product evolved.

## Continuous Research

I identified a gap in how the team was gathering and acting on user feedback, and designed a continuous research process to address it. This meant defining the structure from scratch: how tests were planned, how findings were documented, and how insights were made accessible to stakeholders rather than siloed in research notes.

I led the process end-to-end — running multiple rounds of testing and analysis — and built a documentation framework that gave the broader team a clear, ongoing view of what users were telling us. Three designers used it regularly; the remaining two drew on it as needed.

To make the existing body of research usable, I also restructured the team's Dovetail workspace with a comprehensive tagging system. Research that can't be found doesn't inform decisions — migrating and retagging the existing data made it significantly easier to surface relevant findings when new design questions came up.

## Reflection

Blick is where I moved from executing decisions to owning them — and from working alone to making the people around me more effective. That showed up across the work: the design system rebuild, the research practice, and mentoring an apprentice designer. Less about teaching tools, more about helping someone develop a way of thinking about design problems. The instinct connecting all three was the same: identify what's missing, build something that will last.
