Designing the citizen-facing experience for Switzerland's national e-ID programme — from onboarding to credential presentation — across iOS and Android.

## The Work

swiyu is Switzerland's official digital identity wallet — a mobile application that allows citizens to store, manage, and present legally valid credentials without relying on physical documents. The goal was to make a complex, security-critical experience feel simple, accessible, and trustworthy for the full breadth of the Swiss population.

I contributed across the entire design lifecycle within a cross-functional team working in a SAFe agile environment. My work spanned qualitative research, UX concept development, native mobile UI design for iOS and Android, accessibility compliance, design system maintenance, and close collaboration with engineering through handoff and implementation.

## Contributions

Government identity projects are some of the hardest UX problems to work on. The users are everyone — from digital natives to elderly residents with limited smartphone experience. The stakes are high: errors in this context aren't inconvenient, they're potentially exclusionary. And the regulatory constraints are real, not theoretical; every design decision had to be traceable back to legal frameworks and internally aligned across stakeholders who held fundamentally different mental models of what the product should communicate.

There were no established patterns to follow. We were designing a first-of-its-kind experience for Switzerland, which meant the team had to define conventions — not borrow them.

## Featured: Communicating Trust Levels to Everyday Users

One of the projects where I had end-to-end ownership — from research through to final UI — was designing the trust level indicator: a component that communicates the degree of assurance behind a credential or verifier directly to the user at the moment it matters most.
